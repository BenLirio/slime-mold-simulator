Physarum polycephalum — a single-celled organism that independently rediscovered Tokyo's rail network. This simulation grows the same way: particles follow chemical trails, amplifying paths toward food until the network self-organizes.

In 2010, researchers Tero, Takagi, Saigusa and colleagues placed oat flakes at the locations of 36 major stations around Tokyo. The slime mold grew a transport network remarkably similar to the real Yamanote rail line — built by engineers over a century, rediscovered by a brainless cell in 26 hours.
